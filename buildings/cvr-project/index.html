The CVR Project

Overview

The CVR project started when my sister Cambree and I wanted to play creative Minecraft together. Â Within a day, the world quickly evolved into many elaborate builds including a dense vertical city.


The Wall

The wall visually divides Cambree's builds from mine.

My side of the wall has two tiers to allow space for trees and plants.

Surrounded by elevated walkways is a small rocky area with an artificial creek running through it.

Cambree's side of the wall is coated in Jungle wood planks to cover up the bright white quarts used on my side.


Design Choices

Since the structures incorporate plain white quarts and sandstone, I made the roofs orange to attract attention.

I try to add balconies wherever I can. They both serve an aesthetic purpose by breaking up the flat nature of blocky buildings and a practical purpose by giving tenants fresh air.

Inspired by zoo exhibits, this underground tunnel segment of the walkway features an aquatic pond for fish with a railing along the path. This room off to the side of the tunnel helps reduce claustrophobia by bringing railings(usually an outdoor feature) inside.

More Images

Although not yet completed, this upper section will be more enclosed/sterile using glass in areas that would have otherwise been left open. It also features a skywalk for connection at the highest levels of the city.

Atop the mountain, Cambree and I worked together to create identical viewpoints. One is on either side of the wall, they are connected by two gates that each only allow for one-way travel. This allows an underground computer to track how many people are on each side of the wall..

Because the city is built into a mountainy basin, almost all the balconies and viewpoints face inward to other buildings.
